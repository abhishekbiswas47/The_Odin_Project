Rasmalai

Description

Rasmalai is a delectable Indian dessert featuring soft cottage cheese balls soaked in a creamy, fragrant milk syrup. With its delicate texture and rich flavors of cardamom and saffron, Rasmalai offers a truly indulgent experience. Served chilled and garnished with nuts, this sweet treat is a celebration of Indian culinary delights, bringing pure delight to every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups milk
  • 1/4 cup condensed milk
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • Saffron strands (optional)
  • Chopped nuts for garnish (pistachios, almonds)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring 2 cups of milk to a boil. Once it boils, remove from heat and add lemon juice. Stir gently until the milk curdles and the whey separates from the solids.
  2. Pour the curdled milk into a cheesecloth-lined sieve or muslin cloth. Rinse it under cold water to remove any lemony flavor.
  3. Squeeze out the excess water from the cheese cloth and hang it for 30 minutes to drain any remaining whey.
  4. Meanwhile, in a separate pot, bring water and sugar to a boil to make the sugar syrup.
  5. Once the sugar syrup is ready, gently drop the cheese balls (paneer) into the boiling syrup. Cover and cook for about 10 minutes on medium heat.
  6. In another pan, heat 4 cups of milk and bring it to a simmer. Add condensed milk, cardamom powder, and saffron strands (if using). Stir well and simmer for 5 minutes.
  7. Remove the cooked paneer balls from the sugar syrup and gently squeeze out any excess syrup.
  8. Transfer the paneer balls (rasgullas) to the simmering milk mixture (rabri) and cook for another 5 minutes.
  9. Remove from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  10. Garnish with chopped nuts and refrigerate for a few hours before serving.
