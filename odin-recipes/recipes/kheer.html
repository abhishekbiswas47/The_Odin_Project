Kheer (Rice Pudding)

Description

Kheer is a creamy and aromatic rice pudding that is commonly made in Indian households during festivals and special occasions. The rice is cooked in milk until it becomes soft and the mixture thickens to a rich and creamy consistency. The addition of sugar, nuts, and aromatic spices like cardamom adds a delightful flavor to this comforting dessert. Whether enjoyed warm or chilled, Kheer is a beloved sweet treat that brings joy to every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup basmati rice
  • 4 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup mixed nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios), chopped
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • Saffron strands (optional)
  • Raisins for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes.
  2. Drain the rice and set it aside.
  3. In a large heavy-bottomed pan, bring the milk to a boil over medium heat.
  4. Add the drained rice to the boiling milk and stir well.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and simmer the mixture, stirring occasionally, until the rice is cooked and the milk thickens. This process may take about 30-40 minutes.
  6. Add sugar, chopped nuts, cardamom powder, and saffron strands (if using). Stir well to combine.
  7. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, stirring continuously, until the sugar dissolves completely and the kheer thickens further.
  8. Remove from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  9. Garnish with raisins and refrigerate for a couple of hours before serving. Kheer can be enjoyed warm or chilled.
